## Where things stand today

Korea has 1,300 breweries. Thousands of varieties of *jeontongju* — traditional alcohol — including makgeolli, cheongju, yakju, distilled soju, fruit wines, and countless variations between them. A small brewery alone makes five to ten kinds of drinks on average.

Yet this richness sits **scattered.** Brewery information lives across SNS posts and news articles. Product data differs between e-commerce sites. User reviews are buried in Instagram and personal blogs.

When someone asks *"What is this drink? How did others enjoy it? What's similar?"* — there's no single place that answers. There are good e-commerce apps and thoughtful curation boxes, but **a unified information hub for discovering, tracking, and sharing** has been missing.

## What Sooly is filling

Sooly is an information hub for Korean alcohol. The way [Vivino](https://www.vivino.com) (50M users) holds that role for wine, and [Untappd](https://untappd.com) (1B check-ins) holds it for beer — Sooly is building that for *jeontongju*.

The catalog currently has 800+ products and 400+ breweries. Users browse by category, region, and ABV; record what they've tried with check-ins; and see other people's ratings, pairings, and taste profiles alongside.

Three core features:

1. **Discover** — A nationwide catalog of breweries and products. Browse by category, region, or alcohol content, and understand each drink alongside its brewery's story.
2. **Track** — User reviews of what you've tried. Star ratings, food pairings, short notes, and a six-axis taste profile, all from anyone. Over time, this becomes your personal Korean alcohol library.
3. **Share** — See the *current state* of Korean alcohol through other users' reviews, ratings, pairings, and taste-profile trends. How a drink's average rating shifts over time, what foods pair with it most often, where collective taste evaluations converge — the meaning grows as data accumulates.

## Why a Korean alcohol hub matters

Three reasons.

**First, the market is growing but the entry point is missing.** Korea's traditional alcohol market shows double-digit annual growth, but there's no natural path for new users to *discover* Korean alcohol and continuously *try* a variety of it. A good entry point directly contributes to market growth.

**Second, most breweries lack IT and marketing resources.** Of the 1,300 breweries, only a fraction have a maintained official website. The infrastructure to bring brewery stories and products to consumers is largely empty. Sooly provides that infrastructure on their behalf, and is building tools so breweries can manage their own pages directly.

**Third, English-language information is dramatically lacking.** Through K-culture, demand for Korean alcohol from overseas Koreans and international fans is growing fast — yet *places where Korean alcohol can be discovered and understood in English* barely exist. Sooly is built bilingually (Korean and English) from the ground up to fill this gap together.

## Why now

Online sales of *jeontongju* have been **legal since 2017**. General alcohol still cannot be shipped to homes in Korea, but traditional alcohol is the exception — making it the only category where an information hub and marketplace can naturally combine.

This legal opening, meeting the global attention K-culture has earned, is what makes the timing right.

## What's next

Content and features will accumulate one by one.

- **Brewery visit notes** — Starting with the ones we've personally been to.
- **Category guides** — Introductions to makgeolli, the world of *cheongju*, distilled soju in one read, and so on.
- **Korean alcohol primer in English for K-culture fans** — A piece for those approaching for the first time.
- **Brewery Verified Plan** — Beyond letting breweries manage their own pages, a paid toolkit offering **priority placement for new releases**, **traffic and search-keyword analytics**, and a **dashboard for user reviews, pairings, and taste-profile trends**. [Interested breweries are welcome to inquire here](https://sooly.co.kr/for-breweries).

Sooly started in the hands of one person who loves Korean alcohol. It's not a flashy beginning, but **one piece at a time, slowly and continuously, it will keep growing.**
