## Three Days at Yangjae AT Center

The Korea Makgeolli Expo (MAXPO) is the nation's largest makgeolli (a milky, lightly fizzy rice wine) fair, held every spring at the Yangjae AT Center. The 2026 event marked its fifth installment. For three days, from May 15th to 17th, almost everything related to Korean alcohol – including makgeolli, local specialty spirits, brewing experiences, and pairing snacks and food – gathered in one space.

I visited on the last day, May 17th.

There was a vast array of jeontongju (traditional Korean alcohol), and it was delightful to see food booths alongside the alcohol. I also had many great conversations and shared glasses with fellow alcohol enthusiasts.

Here are five drinks that particularly impressed me after personal tasting, and which I actually purchased:

---

## 1. Minju Suldoga — Concerto No. 1 / Dokripgun Makgeolli

![민주술도가 콘체르토 1번과 독립군막걸리](/blog/makgeolli-expo-2026/minju-suldoga-dokrip-concerto.jpg)

*Pocheon, Gyeonggi Province · Agricultural Corporation Minju Suldoga*

Minju Suldoga is a handcrafted makgeolli brewery located in Pocheon, Gyeonggi Province. I tasted two varieties at this expo: **Concerto No. 1** and **Dokripgun Makgeolli**.

**Concerto No. 1** (6% ABV, 500ml) is Minju Suldoga's signature beverage. It's a **samyangju (a three-stage fermentation method)**, crafted using rice nuruk, which is a modern reinterpretation of *heuteum nuruk (a traditional scattered-grain nuruk technique)* from Joseon-era texts, and Yeast No. 1, isolated from traditional nuruk by the Korea Food Research Institute. This brewing method involves adding nuruk and rice three times, not just once. In 2025, it won the **Grand Prize (Daejang)** in the takju (unfiltered rice wine) category at the 3rd Gyeonggi Alcohol Fair.

**Dokripgun Makgeolli** is a limited-edition premium line. At **17% ABV**, it's a very high-proof drink for an unfiltered rice wine, setting it apart from typical makgeolli.

> *Concerto No. 1* — This drink had a noticeable refreshing and sweet pear flavor. It also offered a sweetness reminiscent of cool sikhye (sweet rice punch).

> *Dokripgun Makgeolli* — It felt substantial, with the 17% ABV making a strong impression. It was as if the spirit of the independence fighters had been imbued into its flavor.

## 2. Bomnae Brewery — Yeolsu Hangang Makgeolli Yu 13

![봄내양조장 열수한강막걸리유 13 유자](/blog/makgeolli-expo-2026/bomnae-yeolsu-hangang-yu13-yuja.jpg)

*Chuncheon, Gangwon Province · Agricultural Corporation Bomnae Brewery*

Bomnae Brewery is located in Chuncheon, Gangwon Province. *Bomnae* is an old name for Chuncheon.

The drink I brought home from this expo was **Yeolsu Hangang Makgeolli Yu 13** — *Yeolsu* is an old name for the Hangang River, *Yu* refers to yuja (citrus fruit), and *13* is the alcohol percentage. It's a **13% ABV makgeolli with yuja added to a lotus leaf base**. Bomnae Brewery's *Yeolsu Hangang* line is a signature series comprising eight types of makgeolli (lotus, dark, omija, yuja × 13% ABV/15.5% ABV + 12% ABV) and four types of soju (31%, 42%, 53%, 53% oak), with lotus leaf forming the base of the line.

> The aroma of lotus leaf wasn't prominent, but there was a clean flavor reminiscent of it. The yuja aroma layered naturally and harmoniously, without being artificial — it was absolutely delicious.

## 3. Baegu Brewery — Baessi Makgeolli

![배구양조장 배씨막걸리](/blog/makgeolli-expo-2026/baegu-baessi-makgeolli.jpg)

*Pocheon, Gyeonggi Province · A brewery started by two makgeolli school alumni*

Baegu Brewery is located in Pocheon, Gyeonggi Province. It was started by two alumni from makgeolli school and has recently been gaining buzz as a *hot makgeolli*. They produce two varieties: **Baessi Makgeolli** (9% ABV, premium) and **Gussi Makgeolli**.

> Baessi Makgeolli offered a rich sweetness. Both a *refreshing pear-like sweetness* and a *sweet apple-like sweetness* came through simultaneously. It was thick and had a heavy, deep sweetness from the rice as its base.

---

## 4. Jangan Brewery — Jangan Nuruk

![장안양조장 장안누룩](/blog/makgeolli-expo-2026/jangan-nuruk.jpg)

*Dongdaemun-gu, Seoul · A community brewery combined with urban regeneration*

Jangan Brewery is a **community brewery** located in Dongdaemun-gu, Seoul. It operates in a small space of about 15 pyeong (approx. 500 sq ft), integrated with an urban regeneration project.

The drink I brought home this time was **Jangan Nuruk** — the brewery's signature. This 9% ABV makgeolli is brewed using **traditional nuruk** exclusively, with no artificial sweeteners, colors, or additives whatsoever. It's characterized by ripe fruit aromas, a subtle floral scent, and a dense, sweet flavor. In addition to Jangan Nuruk, their lineup also includes a drink called *Jangan Sai*, and another with a lively tartness like passion fruit.

> This makgeolli featured the deep sweetness found in grapes or the inside of a Korean melon, along with a creamy texture. There was a slightly bright floral note, and a light acidity flowing from it. Overall, it wasn't heavy but had just the right creaminess — making it a very well-balanced drink.

## 5. Jungwondang — Cheongmyeongju Takju

![중원당 청명주 탁주](/blog/makgeolli-expo-2026/jungwondang-cheongmyeong-takju.jpg)

*Chungju, Chungcheongbuk-do · Chungcheongbuk-do Intangible Cultural Heritage No. 2 Cheongmyeongju*

Jungwondang is a brewery located in Chungju, Chungcheongbuk-do. It is the birthplace of **Cheongmyeongju**, designated as Chungcheongbuk-do Intangible Cultural Heritage No. 2.

Cheongmyeongju (淸明酒) is an alcohol traditionally brewed and consumed around *Cheongmyeong* (one of the 24 solar terms, around April 5th in the Gregorian calendar). Following a traditional recipe from the old text 《Hyangjeonrok (香薦錄)》, it is fermented and aged at **low temperatures for approximately 100 days**, using whole wheat and glutinous rice as ingredients and groundwater from the Namhan River. In 2022, it was awarded the **President's Award** (the highest honor) at the Korea Traditional Alcohol Competition.

Cheongmyeongju is available in two forms: *yakju (refined rice wine, 17% ABV)* and *takju (unfiltered rice wine, 12% ABV)*. The one I encountered at the expo was **Cheongmyeongju Takju** (12% ABV, 375ml) — the unfiltered form of the same alcohol.

> The umami and nutty flavors from the glutinous rice were impressive. A refreshing acidity was accompanied by a subtle fruit flavor, much like a fruit wine, and a dense texture supported the entire drink.
