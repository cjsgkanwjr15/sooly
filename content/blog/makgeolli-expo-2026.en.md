## The Three Days at Yangjae AT Center

The Korea Makgeolli Expo (MAXPO) is the largest makgeolli (a milky, lightly fizzy rice wine) exhibition in Korea, held every spring at Yangjae AT Center. 2026 marked its fifth edition. From May 15th to 17th, for three days, almost everything related to Korean alcohol — including makgeolli, regional specialty liquors, brewing experiences, and even snacks and food — gathered in one place.

I visited on the last day, May 17th.

There were so many types of jeontongju (traditional Korean alcohol), and with food stalls alongside them, it was a delightful experience in many ways. It was also a place where I could share drinks and stories with fellow alcohol enthusiasts.

Among them, I'd like to share five alcohols that impressed me after I personally tasted them, and which I ultimately purchased.

---

## 1. Minju Suldoga — Concerto No. 1 / Dokripgun Makgeolli

![민주술도가 콘체르토 1번과 독립군막걸리](/blog/makgeolli-expo-2026/minju-suldoga-dokrip-concerto.jpg)

*Sohul-eup, Pocheon, Gyeonggi Province · Minju Suldoga Agricultural Corporation*

Minju Suldoga is a craft makgeolli brewery in Pocheon, Gyeonggi Province. At this expo, I encountered two of their alcohols — **Concerto No. 1** and **Dokripgun Makgeolli**.

**Concerto No. 1** (6% ABV, 500ml) is Minju Suldoga's signature alcohol. It's a **samyangju** (a three-stage fermentation method) crafted using *rice nuruk* (traditional Korean fermentation starter) that reinterprets the *heuteum nuruk* found in Joseon-era texts, along with *Yeast No. 1*, isolated from traditional nuruk by the Korea Food Research Institute. This brewing method involves adding nuruk and rice in three stages, making it time and labor-intensive. In 2025, it won the Grand Prize (the highest award) in the takju category at the 3rd Gyeonggi Alcohol Competition.

**Dokripgun Makgeolli** is a limited-edition premium line. At **17% ABV**, it's a very high-alcohol takju, distinct from typical makgeolli.

> *Concerto No. 1* — This alcohol offered a distinct coolness and sweetness reminiscent of pear. It also brought a refreshing sweetness, like drinking sikhye (a traditional Korean sweet rice beverage).

> *Dokripgun Makgeolli* — It was heavy-bodied, and the 17% ABV was strongly palpable. It felt as if "the spirit of the independence fighters had been imbued into its flavor."

## 2. Bomnae Brewery — Yeolsu Hangang Makgeolli Yu 13

![봄내양조장 열수한강막걸리유 13 유자](/blog/makgeolli-expo-2026/bomnae-yeolsu-hangang-yu13-yuja.jpg)

*Chuncheon, Gangwon Province · Bomnae Brewery Agricultural Corporation*

Bomnae Brewery is located in Chuncheon, Gangwon Province. *Bomnae* is the old name for Chuncheon.

The alcohol I brought back from this expo was **Yeolsu Hangang Makgeolli Yu 13** — *Yeolsu (洌水)* is the old name for the Hangang River, *Yu* refers to yuzu, and *13* is the alcohol percentage. It's a **13% ABV makgeolli with yuzu added, based on lotus leaf**. Bomnae Brewery's *Yeolsu Hangang* line is a signature series comprising eight types of makgeolli (Lotus · Dark · Oh · Yu × 13% / 15.5% ABV + 12% ABV) and four types of soju (31% · 42% · 53% · 53% Oak), with *Lotus (yeonip)* forming the base of the line.

> The lotus leaf aroma wasn't prominent, but there was a clean taste reminiscent of lotus leaf. Layered naturally and not artificially, the yuzu fragrance combined beautifully — it was truly delicious.

## 3. Baegu Brewery — Baessi Makgeolli

![배구양조장 배씨막걸리](/blog/makgeolli-expo-2026/baegu-baessi-makgeolli.jpg)

*Pocheon, Gyeonggi Province · A brewery started by two classmates from makgeolli school*

Baegu Brewery is located in Pocheon, Gyeonggi Province. It's a brewery started by two classmates from makgeolli school, and it's reportedly gaining buzz recently as a *hot new makgeolli*. The brewery's name comes from the two characters *Bae* and *Gu*, and they produce two different alcohols — **Baessi Makgeolli** (9% ABV, premium) and **Gussi Makgeolli**.

> Baessi Makgeolli offered a rich sweetness. Both the *refreshing sweetness of pear* and the *sweetness of apple* came through simultaneously. It was thick, with the deep sweetness of rice forming a weighty base.

## 4. Jangan Brewery — Jangan Nuruk

![장안양조장 장안누룩](/blog/makgeolli-expo-2026/jangan-nuruk.jpg)

*Dongdaemun-gu, Seoul · A community brewery combined with urban regeneration*

Jangan Brewery is a **community brewery** located in Dongdaemun-gu, Seoul. It operates in a small space of about 15 pyeong (approx. 50 square meters), integrated with an urban regeneration project.

The alcohol I brought back this time was **Jangan Nuruk** — the brewery's signature. It's a 9% ABV makgeolli, brewed **solely with traditional nuruk**, using no artificial sweeteners, colorings, or additives whatsoever. It's characterized by ripe fruit aromas, subtle floral notes, and a rich, sweet flavor. In addition to Jangan Nuruk, their lineup also includes *Jangan Sai* and a vibrant, tangy alcohol reminiscent of passionfruit.

> It was characterized by the deep sweetness found in grapes or the inner part of Korean melons, along with a creamy texture. There was a hint of bright floral scent, and a light acidity flowing from it. Overall, it wasn't heavy but appropriately creamy — a very well-balanced alcohol.

## 5. Jungwondang — Cheongmyeongju Takju

![중원당 청명주 탁주](/blog/makgeolli-expo-2026/jungwondang-cheongmyeong-takju.jpg)

*Chungju, Chungbuk Province · Chungcheongbuk-do Intangible Cultural Heritage No. 2 Cheongmyeongju*

Jungwondang is a brewery located in Chungju, Chungbuk Province. It is the very place that brews **Cheongmyeongju** (clear filtered rice wine), which is designated as Chungcheongbuk-do Intangible Cultural Heritage No. 2.

Cheongmyeongju (淸明酒) is an alcohol traditionally brewed and consumed around *Cheongmyeong* (approximately April 5th of the solar calendar), one of the 24 solar terms. Following a traditional recipe from the ancient text *Hyangjeonrok*, it is fermented and aged for **about 100 days at low temperatures** using whole wheat, glutinous rice, and groundwater from the Namhangang River. This 100-day period is deeply infused into the alcohol's flavor. In 2022, it was awarded the **Presidential Award** (the highest honor) at the Korea Traditional Alcohol Competition.

Cheongmyeongju is released in two forms: *yakju (refined rice wine, clear alcohol, 17% ABV)* and *takju (unfiltered alcohol, 12% ABV)*. The one I encountered at this expo was **Cheongmyeongju Takju** (12% ABV, 375ml) — the unfiltered version of the same alcohol.

> The umami and nutty flavors from the glutinous rice were impressive. A refreshing acidity was followed by a subtle fruity taste, much like fruit wine, and a rich texture underpinned the entire drink.
