## The Current State of Korean Alcohol

Across the country, there are 1,300 breweries and thousands of types of jeontongju (traditional Korean alcohol). This includes makgeolli (a milky, lightly fizzy rice wine), cheongju (clear filtered rice wine), yakju (refined rice wine), distilled soju, fruit wine, and countless variations in between. Even a small brewery typically crafts 5 to 10 different kinds of alcohol.

However, this rich resource is **scattered**. Brewery information is dispersed across individual social media accounts and news articles, product data varies from one e-commerce site to another, and user reviews are buried in Instagram posts and blogs.

When consumers ask questions like, *'What is this alcohol?', 'How did others enjoy it?', 'Are there similar options?'*, there's no single place to find answers. While excellent e-commerce apps and carefully curated subscription boxes exist, the form of an **integrated information hub for discovery, tracking, and sharing** remains empty.

## The Gap Sooly Aims to Fill

Sooly is an information hub dedicated to Korean alcohol. Just as [Vivino](https://www.vivino.com) (50 million users) serves wine and [Untappd](https://untappd.com) (1 billion+ check-ins) serves beer, Sooly steps in as the Korean alcohol version.

Currently, over 800 products and more than 400 breweries are listed in our catalog. Users can explore by category, region, and alcohol content, record the beverages they've tried with check-ins, and view other users' ratings, pairings, and flavor profiles.

Three core features:

1.  **Discover** — A catalog of breweries and products nationwide. Explore by category, region, and alcohol content, understanding each through the brewery's story.
2.  **Track** — User reviews for tried beverages. Anyone can directly leave ratings, food pairings, quick notes, and a 6-axis flavor profile. Over time, this builds into a personal Korean alcohol library.
3.  **Share** — See the *current state* of Korean alcohol through other users' reviews, ratings, pairings, and flavor profile trends. Understand how a drink's average rating changes, what foods it's often paired with, and where people's average taste evaluations converge — the more data accumulates, the deeper the insights become.

## Why a Korean Alcohol Information Hub is Essential

There are three key reasons.

**First, the market is growing, but entry points are scarce.** The jeontongju market shows double-digit annual growth, yet there's no natural path for new users to *discover* Korean alcohol for the first time and continuously *explore* various options. Good entry points directly contribute to market growth.

**Second, most breweries lack IT and marketing resources.** Among the 1,300 breweries, only a few have active official websites, and even those are often not well-maintained. The infrastructure to connect breweries' stories and products with consumers is missing. Sooly provides that infrastructure and is also creating tools for breweries to manage their own pages directly.

**Third, English information is critically lacking.** With the rise of K-culture, demand for Korean alcohol among Koreans abroad and foreigners is rapidly increasing, yet there are very few *places to discover and understand* Korean alcohol in English. Sooly's Korean-English bilingual design aims to fill this gap.

## Why Now?

Since 2017, **online sales of jeontongju have been legal**. While home delivery for general alcoholic beverages is still restricted, jeontongju is an exception — making it the only alcohol category where an information hub can be combined with a marketplace.

This intersection of legality and global interest in K-culture is happening now.

## Looking Ahead

-   **Brewery Visit Reviews** — We'll document our visits, one brewery at a time, starting with those we've personally experienced.

Sooly began in the hands of one person who loves Korean alcohol. While it may not be a grand start, **we will grow steadily, one piece at a time.**
